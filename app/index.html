WHAT IS CCT AND WHAT YOU WILL LEARN?

CCT — Certified cyber security technician. An entry- level program, focused on hands-on practice, with more than 50% of training time dedicated to labs. Dive into the details below.

01. Key Concepts in Cybersecurity

Grasp the Core: Learn the fundamentals of securing digital environments and understand the key concepts that drive the cybersecurity industry.

02. Security Threats, Vulnerabilities and Attacks

Assess & Identify: Master the art of identifying risks, pinpointing weaknesses, and understanding the nature of malicious actions in digital realms.

03. Types of Malware

Unmask Malicious Software: Dive deep into the various types of malware and how they compromise digital security.

04. Authentication and Authorization

Control Access: Understand the intricacies of user identity and manage access permissions effectively.

05. Network and Application Security Design

Build Fortified Structures: Discover the best practices for designing secure digital environments to mitigate threats and vulnerabilities.

Harness the Future: Learn how to exploit modern technology for scalable, secure operations and better threat management.

07. Wireless Network Basics, Encryption and Security

Master Wireless Security: Gain insights into the principles of secure wireless connectivity, from basic network concepts to advanced encryption techniques.

08. Mobile and IoT

Connect & Protect: Navigate through the world of smart devices, focusing on enhanced digital experiences and seamless connectivity without compromising security.

09. Cryptography and Data Security

Guard Your Secrets: Utilize encryption techniques effectively to safeguard sensitive information and ensure data integrity.

10. Network Troubleshooting, Monitoring and Analysis

Solve & Evolve: Develop skills for resolving network performance issues, while proficiently observing and analyzing network activities.

Respond & Analyze: Learn to manage security incidents effectively and delve into the realm of digital evidence analysis and computer forensics.

12. Disaster Recovery and Risk Management

Plan & Mitigate: Equip yourself with the knowledge to plan for resilience, manage disasters, and take a proactive approach to risk mitigation.
